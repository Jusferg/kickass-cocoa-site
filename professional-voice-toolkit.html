Why This Matters

How this connects to your growth inside Kick-A$$ Cocoa.

Voice is more than sound. It is how your ideas arrive, how your confidence is felt, and how your presence is remembered. For many women — especially those navigating spaces where they may feel overlooked, underestimated, or underrepresented — strengthening voice and presence is not just about public speaking. It is about being able to take up space with clarity and intention.

This toolkit is here to help you practice showing up fully: not louder for performance, but clearer, steadier, and more grounded in who you are and what you bring.

Kick-A$$ Cocoa Reflection

Use these prompts after your first lesson or module.

01

Where do I shrink my voice?

Think about situations where you hesitate, soften your ideas, or hold back what you really want to say.

02

When do I feel most powerful when I speak?

Identify the settings, people, or conditions that help your confidence expand instead of contract.

03

What do I want people to feel or remember when I speak?

Clarifying your intention can strengthen both your confidence and your presence.

Practice Exercise

Turn reflection into action.

Write a short message below introducing an idea, opinion, or request you want to express with more confidence this week. Focus on clarity, steadiness, and directness.

Power Phrases

Replace shrinking language with language that lands.

Instead of

“Sorry, I just wanted to add…”

Try

“I’d like to add a perspective here.”

Instead of

“This might be a bad idea…”

Try

“Here’s an approach worth considering.”

Instead of

“I’m not sure if this makes sense…”

Try

“Here’s what I’m seeing.”

Instead of

“Just checking in…”

Try

“Following up on the next step here.”
